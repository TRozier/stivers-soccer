This is how we play at Stivers. This is our blueprint. It is your responsibility to study not only your position, but all positions — because we only succeed as a team.

Study each positional role. When you are ready, take the quiz. Passing with 75% or more earns you freedom from the extra work you will have if not. You fail, you get extra efforts, you try again.

Study guide

Game principles first. Then your position.

Phase 1 — Attacking (In Possession)

Purposeful Directness

• Play forward with intent. Keep the ball purposefully — always looking to progress and attack. Possession for possession’s sake is not our game.

• Stretch the opponent. Create width and depth to open spaces between and behind defensive lines.

• Break defensive lines. Incisive passes, dribbles, and dynamic runs to penetrate the opponent’s shape.

• Speed of play. Move the ball quickly to exploit gaps and disrupt defensive organization.

• Create and finish. Use creativity, off-ball movement, and quick combinations to generate high-quality chances. Occupy near post, back post, penalty spot, and edge of the box. React to second balls.

Phase 2 — Attacking to Defending (A to D)

Immediate, Disciplined Transition

• Immediate reaction. Whoever is closest to the ball presses first — no hesitation.

• 5-second rule. Counter-press aggressively. If the ball is not won back within 5 seconds — reset into defensive shape. The reset is discipline, not failure.

• Reorganize quickly. Transition into compact defensive shape to deny space.

• Force wide. Channel the ball toward less dangerous areas to buy time.

• Anticipate outlets. Identify the opponent’s passing options and cut off key lanes before they are used.

🚨 Universal press triggers — every phase, every position: (1) Heavy touch — react immediately. (2) Defender receiving with back to goal — press now.
Phase 3 — Defending (Out of Possession)

Tactical Discipline with Smart Aggression

• Immediate pressure. Apply controlled aggression to regain possession, led by the player who lost the ball.

• Three defensive shapes. High press — opponent GK or CB building, or we need it urgently late. Mid block — default. Low block — protecting a lead, man down, or significant quality gap.

• The 9 sets the press. If the 9 goes, everyone goes. If the 9 holds, everyone holds. His movement is the trigger — not a call, an action.

• Deny, delay, deflect. Close passing lanes, slow the attack, force play wide or backward. Never concede the central lane willingly.

• Collective responsibility. All players contribute. Long ball over the top — every line drops and funnels toward goal together. Collective or it is chaos.

Phase 4 — Defending to Attacking (D to A)

Exploit Opportunities with Purpose

• Move immediately. The moment the ball is won — move. Give the winner an outlet before they look up.

• Three counter options. Go direct in behind. Play to the 9 to hold. Secure possession and build. Choose fast.

• Scan before the ball arrives. The decision is made before possession is won — not after.

• Vertical progression. Look for players in advanced positions to bypass pressure quickly. Use width to exploit space on the flanks.

• Controlled tempo. Attack with pace when the opportunity is real. Counter not on — secure, build, move into Phase 1.
